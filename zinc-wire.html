ZINC WIRE

Zinc wire is a slender, ductile metallic filament composed primarily of zinc, a naturally occurring element which is known for its highly reactive nature. Zinc wires are manufactured using special high-grade zinc ingots (99.995% pure, according to EN 1179 - "Z1") in various diameters and lengths, which depend on its applications. They are easily shaped and formed into different configurations, with the surface finish varying according to specific manufacturing process and intended use.

FEATURES

Sizes

Zinc wires are available in 1.2-4.75 mm

Purity

Zinc wires are manufactured using LME-approved brands, ensuring 99.995% purity.

Electrical Conductivity

Zinc wires display excellent conductivity, making them ideal for metallizing processes such as arc and flame spraying.

Surface Quality

Zinc wires have a smooth, clean surface that enhances the durability of spray nozzles and provides a consistent coating.

Customization

Zinc wires are available in various diameters and can be tailored to meet specific customer needs.

APPLICATIONS

Metalizing

Zinc wires are used in thermal spraying to create a protective zinc coating on metal surfaces, offering excellent corrosion resistance.

Ship Decks

Zinc metallizing is applied to ship decks to provide a durable, long-lasting, and corrosion-resistant surface, especially in harsh marine environments.

Gas Cylinders

Zinc coating on gas cylinders enhances their safety and durability by preventing corrosion and potential leaks.

Bridges

Zinc metallizing is used to protect steel bridge structures from corrosion caused by environmental factors, ensuring their longevity and structural integrity.

Heavy Machinery and Vehicles

Zinc coating on heavy machinery and vehicles increases their lifespan by protecting them from rust and corrosion, reducing maintenance costs.

Electrical Conductivity

Zinc wire is essential in capacitor manufacturing, as it's used to create conductive electrodes on dielectric films, enhancing the capacitor's ability to store and release electrical energy.
