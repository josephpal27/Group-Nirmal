ZINC RODS

Zinc rods are cylindrical metallic bars primarily composed of zinc, a reactive metal known for its corrosion-resistant properties. They are manufactured using virgin quality (ZN 99.995%, according to EN 1179-Z1) ingots in various diameters and lengths, offering versatility in their applications. Zinc rods are characterised by their silvery-grey colour and malleability, allowing them to be easily machined and shaped. Their surface can exhibit a range of finishes, from bright and shiny to matte and oxidised, depending on the specific manufacturing process and intended use.

APPLICATIONS

Electroplating

Zinc rods play a crucial role in electroplating as sacrificial anodes. They dissolve to protect the metal being plated, offering corrosion resistance and an attractive finish.

Metalising

In metalizing, zinc rods are used in thermal spraying. Molten zinc is sprayed onto a surface, forming a protective coating. This coating enhances the surface's resistance to corrosion and wear.
